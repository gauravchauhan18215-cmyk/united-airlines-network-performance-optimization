Section 02

Methodology & Scope

A reproducible, data-driven approach from raw operational records to an actionable scheduling recommendation.

Data Source

This analysis draws on the U.S. Department of Transportation On-Time Performance dataset (2015), filtered to United Airlines mainline operations. The dataset captures scheduled and actual departure/arrival times, delay causes, and cancellation status at the flight level.

Tools & Process

Data extraction, cleaning, and aggregation were performed in Python using Pandas, with KPI calculation, root-cause delay analysis, and buffer-impact simulation conducted on the resulting route-level summary tables.

Section 03

Root Cause Analysis

Diagnosing delay drivers on the five highest‑volume routes reveals that controllable airline and late‑aircraft delays dominate.

Observation: Airline + Late Aircraft delays account for >85% of controllable delay minutes on SFO → ORD.

Section 04

Recommendation: Schedule Buffer on SFO → ORD

A 10‑minute increase in scheduled block time substantially improves reliability with minimal operational cost.

Simulation Summary

  • Scheduled block time adjusted from 252 min to 262 min (+10 min)
  • Average elapsed time remains 249 min (actual flying time unchanged)
  • On‑time arrival performance improves from 42% to 63%
  • Average arrival delay drops from 14.8 min to 13.8 min
  • Total delay minutes across 43 flights reduced by 206 minutes
